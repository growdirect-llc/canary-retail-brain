# Reference implementations

Canonical illustrative cases — the deployment archetypes the platform was built against, the worked examples that show the operational primitives in motion, the dogfooding posture that validates the architecture in production.

These are not customer testimonials. The platform is in early deployment; specific customer outcomes will be added under signed reference agreements as the program matures. What's here is the **structural reference** — the kinds of operations Canary is designed for, framed honestly as illustrative rather than as customer claims.

## What's NOT here

Customer testimonials. Engagement screenshots. ROI calculators with specific numbers tied to specific named customers.

The platform does not lead with claims it cannot substantiate. What we can substantiate today: the architecture works (we run on it), the benchmarks are real (PwC documented them at scale), the deployment archetype is specific (Murdoch's-class retailers with multi-state regulatory complexity). What we cannot substantiate today: GA-customer outcome metrics. Those come as the program matures and the customers sign off on attributable reference content.
